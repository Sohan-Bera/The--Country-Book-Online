United Nations (UN)


The United Nations (UN) is an intergovernmental organization established in October 24, 1945, after the end of World War 2.
It was created to promote global peace, security and cooperation among countries,
making it nearly universal in its membership. UN works for
maintain international peace and security, protect human rights,
supports sustinable developments, provide humanitarian aids and uphold international law.
