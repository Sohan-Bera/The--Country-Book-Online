United Nations (UN)

The United Nations (UN) is an intergovernmental organization established on October 24, 1945, after the end of World War II. It was created to promote global peace, security, and cooperation among countries, making it nearly universal in its membership.
The UN works to maintain international peace and security, protect human rights, support sustainable development, provide humanitarian aid, and uphold international law.
